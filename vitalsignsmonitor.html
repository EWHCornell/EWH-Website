Low-Cost Vital Signs Monitor

Whether a patient is in acute or long-term care at a hospital, a vital signs monitor is constantly in use, assessing the state of his or her health and alarming doctors if in case of emergency. Though com- mon in every hospital across the US, vital signs monitors are hard to come by in undeveloped countries. Current monitors on the market can cost thousands of dollars, a price clearly unaffordable by many hospitals in these regions. Our goal is to develop a low-cost vital signs monitor, which will sell for approximately $100. This monitor will not only be significantly cheaper, but it will also be more portable than those currently on the market. The measurements include: temperature, pulse oximetry, heart rate, and blood pressure. Our monitor will also include an alarm system to notify doctors im- mediately should a vital sign fall above or below a healthy threshold.
